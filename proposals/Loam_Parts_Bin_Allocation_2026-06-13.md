# Freed-Card Allocation Map — Loam Cycle + Peace Offering teardowns (2026-06-13)

Two decks left the roster and returned their cards to the pool:

- **The Loam Cycle** (Teval, Sultai graveyard/lands) — dismantled 2026-06-08, list archived
  (`archive/old_decklists/the-loam-cycle-20260404-074432.txt`).
- **Peace Offering** (Ms. Bumbleflower, Bant) — dismantled 2026-06-13, redundant 2nd Bumbleflower
  build, list archived (`archive/old_decklists/peace-offering-20251225-182405.txt`).

This maps the high-value freed cards to homes. **Status legend:** **ALLOCATED** = named in a
standing proposal · **PROPOSED** = this round's docs · **CANDIDATE** = a suggestion that still
needs the cross-deck availability/owned-elsewhere check before acting (CLAUDE.md rule) ·
**POOL** = return to the zero-surplus spares pool · **PARK** = premium but no current home.

> Caution: the collection runs many staples at **zero surplus** (`Collection_Master_Status.md`),
> so a "freed" card may already be the single copy another deck depends on. Verify with the
> DeckSafe xlsx / `grep decks/*.txt` before physically moving any CANDIDATE card.

### PARK (premium, no current home)

The **lands-matter / graveyard engine** that *was* Teval's identity has nowhere to go without a
lands/graveyard commander: **Life from the Loam, Crucible of Worlds, Ramunap Excavator, Titania
Protector of Argoth, World Shaper, Azusa, Exploration, Splendid Reclamation, Afterlife from the
Loam, Wight of the Reliquary**, plus the dedicated finishers **Jarad + Lord of Extinction**. Box
these together — they're the seed of a future lands/graveyard build (a Loam successor), not roster
filler. (Crucible is the lone exception with a marginal RS home — see the RS maybeboard.)

## Headline

The Loam teardown is the high-leverage one: it directly feeds the **Grand Design upgrade**
(Craterhoof + ramp), this round's **Radiation Sickness upgrade** (Sylvan/Hedron/Sidisi), and the
**Calamity reanimator lean** (Scarab God + Final Parting) — three of the roster's standing upgrade
proposals, at $0. The lands/graveyard core (PARK) is worth keeping intact as a future build rather
than scattering it.
