# Clive, Ifrit’s Dominant

_Come to me, Ifrit!_

This is a mono-red devotion and hand-cycling deck. Build devotion, discard large hands, refill with Clive, and turn every card into damage, mana, or value. Rinse and repeat.
## Game Plan

Build devotion and mana with cards like:

- [Birgi, God of Storytelling](about:blank)
- [Runaway Steam-Kin](about:blank)
- [Defiler of Instinct](about:blank)
- [Neheb, the Eternal](about:blank)
- [Solphim, Mayhem Dominus](about:blank)
- [Throne of Eldraine](about:blank)
- [The Fire Crystal](about:blank)

Then cast [Clive, Ifrit's Dominant](about:blank), discard the current hand, and draw cards equal to your devotion to red.

The deck turns those discards into value through:

- [Brallin, Skyshark Rider](about:blank)
- [Glint-Horn Buccaneer](about:blank)
- [Magmakin Artillerist](about:blank)
- [Monument to Endurance](about:blank)
- [Surly Badgersaur](about:blank)
- [Containment Construct](about:blank)

Clive’s front side is the main engine. [Ifrit, Warden of Inferno](about:blank) is the secondary plan for removal, burst mana, and eventually another Clive trigger.

### The Payoff

1. [Brallin, Skyshark Rider](about:blank), [Glint-Horn Buccaneer](about:blank), and [Magmakin Artillerist](about:blank) turn every large discard into damage across the table.
    
2. [Orthion, Hero of Lavabrink](about:blank) can create five temporary copies of Clive. Each copy triggers Clive’s ETB before dying to the legend rule, allowing the deck to discard and refill up to five times in one turn.
    
3. [Twinflame](about:blank), [Molten Duplication](about:blank), [Devastating Onslaught](about:blank), [Cursed Mirror](about:blank), and [Storm's Will](about:blank) provide smaller versions of the same Clive-copy line.
    
4. [Fanatic of Mogis](about:blank) converts the same devotion that powers Clive into direct damage to every opponent.
    
5. [Cavalier of Flame](about:blank) gives the deck an unexpected graveyard-based finisher. Clive and the wheel effects naturally discard lands, and [Staff of Compleation](about:blank) can sacrifice (_"technically destroy"_) Cavalier to deal damage to each opponent equal to the number of lands in the graveyard.
    
6. [Underworld Breach](about:blank) allows the deck to reuse [Path of the Pyromancer](about:blank), [Wheel of Fortune](about:blank), and other discard effects for a storm-style finishing turn.
    
7. [Brash Taunter](about:blank) creates a powerful Ifrit line. With [Gratuitous Violence](about:blank), Ifrit fights Taunter for 18 damage, and Taunter deals 36 damage to an opponent.
    
8. [Path of the Pyromancer](about:blank) turns a large hand into damage, mana, and a fresh hand. With a discard payoff in play, it becomes a ritual, wheel, and burn spell at the same time.
    
9. [Solphim, Mayhem Dominus](about:blank), [Twinflame Tyrant](about:blank), [Fated Firepower](about:blank), [Gratuitous Violence](about:blank), and [The Fire Crystal](about:blank) multiply the deck’s damage and turn smaller triggers into lethal ones.
    
10. [Terror of the Peaks](about:blank), [Cavalier of Flame](about:blank), [Neheb, Dreadhorde Champion](about:blank), and the deck’s haste effects allow it to win through combat when the noncombat plan is unavailable.
    

The deck does not rely on one specific combo. Clive repeatedly cycles through fresh hands until one of the deck’s overlapping finishing lines comes together.

One game may end through copied Clive triggers. Another may end through [Fanatic of Mogis](about:blank), a sacrificed [Cavalier of Flame](about:blank), an Ifrit and Brash Taunter fight, an Underworld Breach turn, or one enormous discard.

This is my favorite deck because it rarely wins the same way twice. The core plan remains consistent, but the path to victory changes depending on which pieces appear.

The deck is essentially finished. I only expect to update it slowly when a new card clearly improves the deck without changing the identity or interactions that make it special.
