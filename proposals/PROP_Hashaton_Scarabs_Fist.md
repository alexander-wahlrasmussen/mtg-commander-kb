# Proposal: Hashaton, Scarab's Fist

Status: **not built.** Saved for future consideration.
Drafted: 2026-05-02.

---

## Commander

**Hashaton, Scarab's Fist** (UB). Whenever you cast a noncreature spell, you may tap two untapped creatures you control. When you do, return target creature card from a graveyard to the battlefield as a token copy of it, except it's a 4/4 black Zombie in addition to its other types.

Owned: 3 copies (DRC, foil).

---

## Archetype framing

**Not strictly a reanimator deck.** The token is always 4/4, which means:

- **Bodies don't matter.** Cheating in Emrakul or Blightsteel as a 4/4 is wasted. Stat-based fatties are off-axis.
- **ETBs and static abilities are the entire point.** You want creatures whose value lives in *what they do when they enter*, not how big they are.
- **Classic reanimation (Reanimate, Animate Dead, Necromancy) is off-axis.** Those put the real card back, which doesn't trigger or interact with Hashaton's ability. They become a parallel cheat-in line, not a synergy.
- **Engine = noncreature spell density + multi-graveyard fuel.** Closer to a Brago/Roon ETB-loop or a spellslinger value engine than a Buried Alive → big body pile.

Right archetype label: *spellslinger ETB-cheat engine that uses graveyards as a target list.*

---

## Distinctiveness check

Per `REF_Bracket_3_House_Rules.md`, archetype overlap with the existing roster is grounds for rejection. Two existing Dimir decks to clear:

- **Curse of the Scarab (The Scarab God).** Different lane. Scarab God is mill → pay-mana → tribal aggro with scry payoffs. Hashaton is spell-trigger ETB-copy engine. Genuinely different play patterns despite both being "Dimir zombie tokens from yards."
- **Crystal Sickness (Golbez).** Partial overlap on the headline action ("a big creature comes out of a graveyard onto the battlefield"). Engine and triggers differ. Survives the distinctiveness test only if the build leans *hard* into the spellslinger / ETB-loop identity (untap shells, cheap cantrips, multi-yard mill) rather than building a graveyard pile.

**Verdict:** marginal. Buildable as distinct, but easy to drift into Golbez-overlap if construction is sloppy.

## Power ceiling from collection

**Likely 17+/20 elite range.** Key Dimir staples already owned:

- Tutors / draw: Demonic Tutor, Vampiric Tutor, Mystical Tutor, Necropotence, Bolas's Citadel, Rhystic Study, Mystic Remora, Notion Thief, Opposition Agent
- Removal / interaction: Toxic Deluge, Cyclonic Rift, Force of Will, Mana Drain, Yawgmoth's Will
- ETB-stacked targets to copy: Sepulchral Primordial, Massacre Wurm, Grave Titan, Consecrated Sphinx, Sheoldred (both versions), Sphinx of the Second Sun, Liliana Dreadhorde General
- Reset / wrath: Living Death (multiple)
- Yard-fill: Buried Alive, Entomb, Victimize, Persist

**Caveat:** most of these are at zero surplus across the existing roster (see `Collection_Master_Status.md`). Building Hashaton physical means cannibalizing 1–2 elite decks every play session. Proxy build is cheap; physical sleeve-up has real logistics cost.

---

## Construction direction (if/when built)

Lean into the spellslinger / ETB-loop identity to defend distinctiveness from Golbez:

- High noncreature spell density — cantrips, cheap interaction, ramp instants/sorceries
- Untap effects — Seedborn Muse, Wilderness Reclamation analogues, Pemmin's-style auras on Hashaton
- Multi-graveyard mill (Tasha's Hideous Laughter, Maddening Cacophony) — fuels the target pool with opponents' creatures, not just yours
- ETB-copy targets prioritized over fatties — value engines, not voltron threats
- Classic reanimation as a *secondary* line at most, or omitted entirely to stay distinct from Golbez
