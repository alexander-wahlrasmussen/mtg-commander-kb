# Eldrazi Stampede Chaos — Pilot Primer

**Commander:** Maelstrom Wanderer (Temur / GUR)
**Archetype:** Ramp / cascade / "huge stuff" · **Bracket:** 3
**Full detail:** see `Eldrazi_Stampede_Chaos_Summary.md`. This page is for piloting at the table.

---

## The pitch

Ramp hard into a wall of enormous creatures and cascade/free-cast effects. Maelstrom Wanderer gives **all your creatures haste**, so everything you cheat into play attacks the same turn. Chain free-casts, strip opponents' boards with Annihilator and exile-on-cast triggers, and close with combat or a Craterhoof alpha. The kills want a creature already down or a chain to develop — this is a mid-speed beatdown, not a turn-4 combo.

## How you win

- **Wanderer cascade dump (primary):** cast for {5}{G}{U}{R}; two cascades each hit a ~6–7 MV creature *with haste* (Avenger flood, Etali, Bane of Bala Ged, Sire of Seven Deaths). Turn-of-cast damage typically 15–25. Engine-online → kill in 1–2 turns.
- **Craterhoof Behemoth alpha:** build wide (Avenger Plants, Rampaging Baloths Beasts, Tendershoot Saprolings), then Craterhoof gives the team +X/+X and trample — lethal from 8+ creatures.
- **Ghalta + Eldrazi pile:** Ghalta floors to {G}{G} off a couple of big bodies; Annihilator + trample close 1–2 turns later.
- **Backups:** Ulamog Ceaseless mill (slow but uncounterable); Sunbird's Invocation chain off hardcast bombs.

## Mulligan — what to keep

Looking for: **ramp that reaches 6+ mana by T4–5**, plus a payoff or an engine piece (Wanderer, Conduit of Ruin, Sunbird's).

- **Keep:** 2+ ramp pieces + a threat or a cascade engine.
- **Toss:** no-land hands; all-bombs with no ramp to cast them.

## Turn-by-turn

1. **T1–3:** ramp aggressively — 19 sources, prioritize the ones that fix colors.
2. **T4–6:** Maelstrom Wanderer (cascade ×2), or hardcast Conduit of Ruin (tutors an Eldrazi to the top + discounts your next creature), or Sunbird's Invocation.
3. **T6+:** chain big casts — each triggers Sunbird, Garruk draws, Selvala mana. Annihilator and exile-on-cast strip boards. Close via combat.

## Don't-miss rulings

- **Cascade requires MV *strictly less than 8*.** Wanderer **cannot** cascade into the Eldrazi titans (Ulamogs 10/11, Kozileks 10), Craterhoof (8), or Ghalta (12) — those must be hardcast. It *can* hit Avenger (7), Etali (6), Selvala's Stampede (6), and lower.
- **Sunbird's Invocation triggers ONLY on spells cast from hand** — cascade hits, Aetherworks / Etali / Creative Technique free-casts, and Selvala's Stampede ETBs do NOT trigger it.
- **Both Ulamogs are indestructible** (survive non-exile wipes). Ulamog Infinite Gyre and Kozilek Butcher self-shuffle from the graveyard; **Ulamog Ceaseless Hunger does NOT self-recur** — once milled or exiled it's gone, so hardcast it or keep it in hand.
- **Void Winnower:** while it's on the battlefield, opponents can't cast *even-MV* spells (2/4/6 removal and counters) AND can't block with even-MV creatures.
- **Conduit of Worlds is one-spell-per-turn-total** when you grave-cast (not a bonus cast), and only at sorcery speed if you haven't cast a spell yet that turn.
- **Creative Technique's Demonstrate lets an opponent copy it too** (you resolve first).
- **Decimate needs a legal target in all four categories** (artifact + creature + enchantment + land) or it can't be cast.

## Threats & timing

- **How you lose:** **no hard counterspells despite blue.** Kozilek the Great Distortion's discard-counter (MV-match) is the only stack interaction — combo decks that go off T4–6 pre-empt your T6–8 kill window. Disrupt their pieces early.
- **Cyclonic Rift / Farewell are devastating** — recursion is thin, recovery takes 3+ turns. Re-ramp and re-deploy from hand.
- **Politically loud** — Annihilator triggers and 12/12 Eldrazi draw the table's fire. Expect to be the early target once the engine fires.
- **Goldfish T6–8.** Strong against creature aggro (Void Winnower wall, lifelink/ward bodies, token floods).
