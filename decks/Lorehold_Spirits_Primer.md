# Lorehold Spirits — Pilot Primer

**Commander:** Quintorius, History Chaser (Boros / RW) — *Legendary Planeswalker*
**Archetype:** Graveyard-cycling Spirit tokens · **Bracket:** 3
**Full detail:** see `Lorehold_Spirits_Summary.md`. This page is for piloting at the table.

---

## The pitch

The graveyard *is* the token engine. Whenever one or more cards leave your graveyard, Quintorius makes a 3/2 Spirit — so every recursion piece is a token producer. Anointed Procession doubles each Spirit; Purphoros pings 2 per Spirit ETB. Feed the yard with discard and deaths, fire it with recursion, multiply it, and close with a Quintorius ult + Akroma's Will alpha strike or a Purphoros burn turn.

## How you win

- **Quintorius −4 + Akroma's Will (primary):** −4 gives your Spirits double strike + vigilance; Akroma's Will adds flying / lifelink / indestructible / protection from each color — an alpha strike that survives wraths and removal. Closes T7–9.
- **Purphoros + Anointed Procession + a recursion turn:** each static trigger = 2 Spirits = 4 damage to each opponent. Non-combat; works through tap-down and chump blockers.
- **Moonshaker Cavalry burst:** ETB gives the team flying + a counter — one-turn lethal from a wide board.
- **Backups:** Boros Charm 4-damage finish on a low opponent; Patchwork Banner / Balefire Liege anthem grind.
- **Combo (OFFLINE — pending physical Goblin Bombardment):** Reveillark + Karmic Guide + GB + a ≤2-power creature in yard loops for arbitrary damage and Spirits.

## Mulligan — what to keep

Looking for: **ramp + a discard/recursion piece + a route to Quintorius by T4.**

- **Keep:** ramp + Quintorius + a recursion piece (or Hofri). A doubler (Anointed Procession) or Purphoros is a bonus.
- **Toss:** no-land hands; all-finisher hands with no engine to feed the yard.

## Turn-by-turn

1. **T1:** Sol Ring / Mox Amber; a discard outlet on curve.
2. **T4:** Quintorius commander. First Spirit appears on the next graveyard event.
3. **T5–6:** Hofri Ghostforge or Karmic Guide → first recursion turn. Then Anointed Procession / Purphoros for the multiplier.
4. **Closing turn:** Quintorius +1, +1, then −4 + Akroma's Will → ~30 across the table.

## Don't-miss rulings

- **Quintorius is a PLANESWALKER, not a creature.** He survives creature wipes (Wrath, Tragic Arrogance) but dies to combat damage and direct damage — and **cannot** be protected by Selfless Spirit / Guardian of Faith.
- **The static fires once per *event*** — "one or more cards leave your graveyard" = ONE Spirit, even when two cards leave at once (Reveillark returning two = 1 token, not 2). Many separate events across a turn is how you stack tokens.
- **Hofri exiles dying nontoken creatures — that exile does NOT trigger Quintorius;** the trigger fires later when the Hofri-token's cards hit the graveyard. **Hofri also interferes with the Reveillark loop** (Reveillark gets exiled instead of going to the yard) — remove/sac Hofri first, or use Hofri Spirit tokens as the sac fuel.
- **Table-check ruling:** whether tokens dying (briefly hitting the yard, then ceasing to exist) triggers the static. Mainstream answer is *yes* — meaning dying Hofri Spirits and sacrificed Treasures each fire it. If a judge rules no, several multipliers contract but the engine still runs off real-card recursion.
- **Goblin Bombardment is a placeholder in the `.txt` pending purchase** — treat the combo line as paper until it's physically acquired.

## Threats & timing

- **How you lose:** **graveyard hate.** Rest in Peace / Leyline of the Void / Grafdigger's Cage shut off the recursion shell entirely; only Kami of Ancient Law + Generous Gift answer it — thin coverage, worth a Gamble tutor when GY hate is on the table.
- **Zero stack interaction (Boros).** A counterspell on your kill turn can't be stopped — but Akroma's Will (protection/indestructible) and Teferi's Protection (phase-out) dodge interaction differently.
- **Cyclonic Rift is worst case** — bounces Hofri and recursion creatures, Spirit tokens vanish; ~3 turns to recover. Teferi's Protection is the pod-killer save if held.
- **Goldfish T7–9**, mid-speed but resilient — Quintorius survives creature wipes and the recursion shell rebuilds from any single reset.

*No reskins — the deck runs under printed names (Quintorius's text is user-supplied custom, but it isn't a Universes Beyond alias).*
