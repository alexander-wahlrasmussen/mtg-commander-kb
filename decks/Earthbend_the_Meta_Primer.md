# Earthbend the Meta — Pilot Primer

**Commander:** Toph, the First Metalbender (Naya / RGW)
**Archetype:** Lands + artifacts + earthbend / +1/+1 counter snowball · **Bracket:** 3
**Full detail:** see `Earthbend_the_Meta_Summary.md`. This page is for piloting at the table.

---

## The pitch

Toph makes **every nontoken artifact you control a land**, so each artifact you drop fires your whole landfall suite. Then **earthbend** animates lands into counter-stacked creatures, counter amplifiers multiply the pile, and you close by turning that board into lethal — infect, burn, or extra combats. The board grows every turn and shrugs off most removal because animated lands return when they die.

## How you win

- **Triumph of the Hordes (fastest closer):** {2}{G}{G} gives your team +1/+1, trample, and **infect**. Three earthbent lands at 4+ counters = 15+ infect — **10 poison is lethal regardless of life total.** Needs a developed board, not a combo.
- **Scute Swarm + Purphoros:** at 6+ lands each landfall makes a *copy* of Scute Swarm (exponential). Purphoros pings **2 to each opponent per creature entering** — a dozen Scutes ends the table.
- **All Will Be One burn:** with the amplifier stack a single earthbend 2 becomes 12+ counters → 12+ damage to an opponent, every end step.
- **Moraug / double-strike lands:** extra combats off landfall, or Toph Greatest Earthbender giving land-creatures double strike, swings for lethal.

## Mulligan — what to keep

Looking for: **ramp + Toph castable by T3–4**, plus at least one engine piece (a landfall payoff, an amplifier, or an early earthbend source).

- **Keep:** ramp + Toph; or ramp + Hardened Scales / Lotus Cobra / Scute Swarm with a path to Toph.
- **Toss:** no-land hands; all-finisher hands (Triumph/Purphoros) with no engine to build a board.
- The deck doesn't tutor its finishers — keep hands that **build the engine**, then draw into a closer.

## Turn-by-turn

1. **T1–2:** ramp (Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, Nature's Lore, Farseek); drop cheap engine pieces if you have them.
2. **T3–4:** land Toph. Her end step earthbends one land automatically from here on.
3. **T5–6:** deploy landfall payoffs (Scute Swarm, Felidar Retreat, Evolution Sage) and amplifiers (Doubling Season, Earth Crystal, Cathars' Crusade). Stack counters.
4. **Closing turn:** Triumph of the Hordes on a board of earthbent lands, or Purphoros + a token flood, or let All Will Be One burn the table out.

## Don't-miss rulings

- **Earthbend's return clause fires on death OR exile, NOT on bounce.** A wiped or Path'd land comes back tapped (as a plain land). **Cyclonic Rift is the worst case** — it bounces to hand, so nothing returns.
- **Treasure tokens do NOT become lands.** Toph's static is **nontoken artifacts only** — Smothering Tithe and Bootleggers' Stash Treasures are ramp, not landfall triggers. The Stash *itself* is a nontoken artifact and does become a land.
- **Amplifier ordering: additive before multiplicative.** Hardened Scales (+1) first, *then* Earth Crystal (×2) and Doubling Season (×2). Applying +1 last wastes the doubling.
- **All Will Be One only hits opponents** — target opponent / a creature or planeswalker an opponent controls. It can't target your own stuff or your face. It triggers once per *event* (the final counter total), not once per counter.
- **Commander protection line:** Liquimetal Coating/Torque makes Toph an artifact → her own static makes her a land → earthbend her → she gains the return clause and dodges removal without commander tax. She's a 2/2 while earthbent (0/0 + 2 counters), so she hits softer.
- **The four Toph *variants* have different names** — the legend rule doesn't apply, run them all at once.
- **Annie Joins Up doubles legendary *triggered* abilities only** (Toph's earthbend, Tannuk, etc.) — NOT replacement effects like Doubling Season or Hardened Scales.

## Threats & timing

- **Snowball threat (goldfish T7–9).** Left alone 2–3 turns after Toph lands, the board becomes unmanageable. Expect to be focused.
- **Loves board wipes, fears Cyclonic Rift.** A normal Wrath returns your earthbent lands immediately and leaves Purphoros (indestructible at low devotion); Rift bounces everything to hand and sets you back 3–4 turns.
- **Vulnerable to artifact/enchantment hate.** The amplifier suite (Hardened Scales, Doubling Season, Earth Crystal, Cathars', All Will Be One, Smothering Tithe) is all enchantments/artifacts — Bane of Progress or Vandalblast guts it, and the return clause won't save them.
- **Stack answers are narrow:** Deflecting Swat (any), plus Red Elemental Blast / Pyroblast (blue only). Crop Rotation can fetch Boseiju for instant-speed artifact/enchantment/land removal.
