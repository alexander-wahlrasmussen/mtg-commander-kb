# The Calamity Tax — Pilot Primer

**Commander:** Glarb, Calamity's Augur (Sultai / BUG)
**Archetype:** Lands-matter / big mana / copy payoffs · **Bracket:** 3
**Full detail:** see `The_Calamity_Tax_Summary.md`. This page is for piloting at the table.

---

## The pitch

Ramp into a huge land count, then use Glarb — who lets you **play lands and cast MV4+ spells off the top of your library** — to convert that mana into free spells and card advantage. Kill the table with a scaling X-drain (**Torment of Hailfire**) or a copy-effect blowout (**Kokusho + Rite of Replication**). **Seedborn Muse** turns Glarb from a once-a-turn engine into a once-per-opponent's-turn one.

## How you win

- **Torment of Hailfire — primary:** Cabal Coffers + Urborg + ~10 lands taps for 12+ mana; cast Torment at **X=12+** and the table can't survive the sacrifice/discard/lose-life iterations. One card, needs only mana. Demonic Tutor finds it.
- **Kokusho + kicked Rite of Replication:** 5 legendary copies all die to the legend rule → **25 to each opponent**. Cleanest version: Reanimate Kokusho (1) + kicked Rite (9) = 10-mana table kill.
- **Doppelgang / Mirrorform on Archon of Cruelty:** multiple copy-ETBs strip every opponent of cards, life, and permanents.
- **Backup — value grind:** Seedborn + a flash enabler = 3–4 free MV4+ spells per turn cycle; the table can't out-resource that indefinitely.

## Mulligan — what to keep

Looking for: **lands + ramp** (Sol Ring, signets, land tutors) pointed at 10+ lands, ideally with an extra-land-drop enabler (Exploration / Azusa / Oracle of Mul Daya) and/or Glarb castable on T3.

- **Keep:** ramp-heavy hands; Glarb + fixing; Cabal Coffers + lands.
- **Toss:** spell-heavy no-land hands; a finisher with no mana engine behind it.
- You **do not** need a kill spell in your opener — surveil + ramp will find Torment.

## Turn-by-turn

1. **T1–3:** ramp hard, fetch/tutor lands, deploy an extra-land-drop enabler. Cast Glarb on T3 if able and start surveiling to sculpt the top.
2. **T4–6:** stack lands until Coffers + Urborg come online; resolve Seedborn Muse; hold counter mana while you develop off the top.
3. **Closing turn:** tap Coffers for 12+ → Torment at X=12+, or Reanimate Kokusho + kicked Rite.

## Don't-miss rulings

- **Glarb plays lands and casts MV4+ spells off the top, but normal timing still applies** — lands and sorceries only on your main phase *unless* a flash enabler is out (Valley Floodcaller / High Fae Trickster / Alchemist's Refuge). MV4+ **instants** can be cast off the top at instant speed naturally.
- **Valley Floodcaller does two things:** (1) flash-enables all your noncreature spells, and (2) each noncreature spell **untaps your Frogs — including Glarb** — for another surveil 2, letting you chain through the library in one turn.
- **Seedborn Muse** untaps Glarb and all your lands on *every* opponent's turn — surveil and free casts each turn cycle, and you can hold up counters without skipping development.
- **Cabal Coffers** taps for {B} per Swamp; **Urborg** makes every land a Swamp. 12+ lands = 12+ mana from a single Coffers tap.
- **Kokusho + Rite:** the copies are legendary, all die instantly to the legend rule, each drains 5 → 25 per opponent.
- **Surveil 2 is a free tap ability** — use it to bin sub-4-MV cards so the top of your library stays castable.

## Threats & timing

- **39 lands survive every wipe and Glarb recasts for 3 mana** — board wipes barely slow you, and they clear opponents' boards before a Torment turn.
- **Graveyard hate is the real weakness** (Rest in Peace, Dauthi Voidwalker) — it shuts off Reanimate, Noxious Revival, Lumra, and Icetill Explorer at once. Answers: Force of Vigor, Druid of Purification, Boseiju.
- **The finisher creature pool is thin** (Archon, Kokusho, Gray Merchant, Massacre Wurm). If they get exiled, the copy kills weaken — lean on Torment, which needs no creatures.
- **Interaction is deep** — 5 counters (3 free) and 4 free removal spells. Hold them up; Seedborn means you don't sacrifice development to do it.
- **Don't announce the kill.** A 12+ land board with Coffers and Urborg telegraphs a Torment turn — let it resolve before anyone realizes.
