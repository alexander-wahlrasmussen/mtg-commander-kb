# The Dark Lord's Army — Pilot Primer

**Commander:** Sauron, the Dark Lord (Grixis / UBR)
**Archetype:** Amass engine / drain grinder · **Bracket:** 3
**Full detail:** see `The_Dark_Lord_s_Army_Summary.md`. This page is for piloting at the table.

---

## The pitch

Sauron punishes opponents for *playing the game*. Every spell an opponent casts amasses Orcs 1, growing your single Army token for free. Give the Army evasion to trigger **the Ring**, draw off the Ring, and bleed the table with **Sheoldred + Underworld Dreams + Orcish Bowmasters** — all while sacrificing the Army for value, because it reforms free on the next opponent's spell. You don't make your own sacrifice fodder; opponents make it for you.

## How you win

- **Drain (primary):** Sheoldred + Underworld Dreams + Bowmasters punish every opponent draw (≈3 life each, plus a Bowmasters ping on bonus draws). **Wound Reflection doubles** their end-step loss; Gray Merchant's ETB closes from a stocked board.
- **Voltron Army:** amass stacks across turn cycles — an evasive 10+ power Army ends the game in 2–3 swings.
- **Aristocrats grind:** Dictate of Erebos + Goblin Bombardment + Pitiless Plunderer — sac the Army → each opponent sacrifices → you get a Treasure → Army reforms free. Relentless, not infinite.
- **Backup — graveyard reset:** Living Death / Reanimate / Animate Dead bring back Sheoldred, Gray Merchant, or Yawgmoth.

## Mulligan — what to keep

Looking for: **lands + ramp toward a turn-5–6 Sauron**, plus an early drain piece (Underworld Dreams, Sheoldred) or interaction to survive into the grind.

- **Keep:** ramp into Sauron with a drain enabler or counters to back it up.
- **Toss:** no-land hands; payoff cards with no mana behind them.
- You **do not** need a kill piece in your opener — the engine grinds and the deck draws deep.

## Turn-by-turn

1. **T1–4:** ramp and hold interaction. Once any drain piece is down, opponents start bleeding from their own spells and draws.
2. **T5–6:** cast Sauron (his ward protects him). Every opponent spell now amasses passively.
3. **Mid:** give the Army evasion (Cover of Darkness / Corsairs of Umbar / Rogue's Passage) → Ring tempts you → draw 4 (gain 8 life with Sheoldred out).
4. **Closing:** sac the Army for value as needed (Goblin Bombardment, Skullclamp, Deadly Dispute, Yawgmoth) and finish with Sheoldred drain + Wound Reflection, Gray Merchant, or a Voltron swing.

## Don't-miss rulings

- **The Ring-tempt draw gains *you* life** (Sheoldred fires on *your* 4 draws) — it's a **defensive cushion, not opponent drain.** Opponents bleed when *they* draw, not when you do.
- **The Army reforms for free on the next opponent's spell** — board wipes are only a speed bump, so sacrifice it freely for value.
- **Sauron has ward — sacrifice a legendary artifact or legendary creature** — which makes targeted removal on him very expensive for opponents.
- **Strionic Resonator copies Sauron's amass trigger** (also works on Saruman's amass or Pitiless Plunderer's Treasure trigger) for explosive turns.
- **Barad-dûr is a hidden amass engine:** {X}{X}{B}, {T}: amass Orcs X if a creature died this turn.
- **Yawgmoth proliferates the amass counters** (and his -1/-1 counters double as removal).
- **Reanimation pulls from the graveyard, not amass** — it's a separate recovery tool, not part of the Army loop.

## Threats & timing

- **Durability is the whole point** — the Army's free reform and Sauron's ward mean you recover from wipes trivially. Don't play scared.
- **There's no single explosive finish** — you win over 2–4 turns of accumulating drain (slowest goldfish in the collection, ~T8–10). Manage threat perception so you aren't ganged up on before the drain matters.
- **Fast combo can race the grind.** Lean on the counter suite — 6 counters including Mana Drain, Force of Negation, and Deflecting Swat (free/mana-positive) — and remember **every counterspell an opponent casts at you also amasses.**
- **Graveyard hate** clamps the reset backup, but the primary drain doesn't need the yard, so it's survivable.
